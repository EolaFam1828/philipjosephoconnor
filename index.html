This memorial site exists to preserve a fuller, more human account of Philip Joseph O'Connor's life and to make space for remembrance from every branch of the family and every season of his life.

Philip Joseph O'Connor was born in Salem, Massachusetts, on September 16, 1943, and passed away on March 13, 2026, at his home in Hendersonville, North Carolina, after a long battle with pancreatic cancer.

He grew up in Lynn, Massachusetts, played football, basketball, and baseball, graduated from Essex Aggie High School, and went on to Michigan State University, where he earned degrees in Human Resources, Business Management, and Labor Relations.

Across a career that included General Motors, Raytheon, LTV Vought, Dee Howard Company, EADS Sogerma, Elbit Systems, M7 Aerospace, and 4M Logistics, Philip became known as an executive who cared about people as much as organizations.

A Life Remembered in Full

Like many lives, Philip's was not simple. He was a father, grandfather, executive, sports fan, coach, and a person whose presence shaped people in very different ways. This site keeps room for that complexity instead of flattening it.

He is remembered for his loyalty to Michigan State, his years coaching Little League, his late-in-life love of golf, and small details that stay with families forever, including his enthusiasm for ice cream and banana splits.

He is survived by his children Mark, Bethlee McLaughlin, Brian O'Connor, Jessica Sadoway, and Kerry Stagliano, along with grandchildren, extended family, lifelong friends, and newer companions whose lives were shaped by him.
