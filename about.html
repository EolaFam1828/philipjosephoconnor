Identity and Family Context

This memorial identifies Philip clearly as Philip Joseph O'Connor, also known to many as P.J. O'Connor. He was born in Salem, Massachusetts, grew up in Lynn, and later lived and died in Hendersonville, North Carolina.

He was a father, grandfather, executive, coach, sports fan, and a person whose impact was felt differently across different branches of the family. This site exists in part to keep that full family record from being flattened into something cleaner than real life actually was.

Education and Work

Philip attended Michigan State University after graduating from Essex Aggie High School. He earned a triple major in Human Resources, Business Management, and Labor Relations, and he carried that background into a long professional career.

His work crossed companies that included General Motors, Raytheon, LTV Vought, Dee Howard Company, EADS Sogerma, Elbit Systems, M7 Aerospace, and 4M Logistics. The through-line was people management, leadership, and an ability to move between industrial, aerospace, and executive environments.

Personal Life and Legacy

Outside work, he was shaped by sports, a loyalty to Michigan State, years of Little League coaching, family traditions, and the kind of small habits people remember decades later, including golf and a serious affection for ice cream and banana splits.

His legacy is therefore not just professional. It is also relational. It lives in stories, in absences, in memories, in photographs, and in the way family members and friends continue trying to preserve an honest record of who he was.
